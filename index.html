About

Plant scientist trained across phytochemistry, biochemistry, molecular biology, and analytical chemistry, with research centered on linking genes to metabolites and pathway function.

Scientific profile

My work connects enzyme function with pathway architecture, metabolite diversity, and plant defense. I combine computational candidate discovery with enzyme characterization and metabolite validation to build mechanistic pathway models in crop and medicinal plant systems.

Research direction

I am interested in how plants evolve specialized metabolic networks, how pathway enzymes are coordinated in space and time, and how these systems can be leveraged for crop protection, metabolic engineering, and discovery of useful natural products.

Research Areas

My work spans enzyme discovery, defense metabolism, transcriptome-guided pathway analysis, and metabolomics-driven characterization of plant natural products.

Glyceollin biosynthesis in soybean

I investigated unresolved steps in soybean glyceollin production by integrating multi-transcriptome analysis, coexpression logic, heterologous expression, enzyme assays, and metabolite analysis.

Soybean defense Phytoalexins Pathway discovery

Cytochrome P450 enzymology

A major part of my work focuses on the functional diversification of plant P450s, including CYP73 and CYP81 families, through substrate testing, kinetics, and pathway-context interpretation.

CYP73 CYP81 Enzyme function

Natural product biosynthesis

I am interested in how complex plant metabolites are assembled, regulated, and diversified, including diterpenoid pathways, candidate discovery, and organization of multi-step biosynthetic systems.

Biosynthetic logic Pathway evolution Metabolic innovation

Metabolomics & analytical chemistry

I routinely use LC–MS/MS, high-resolution mass spectrometry, and targeted biochemical workflows to identify pathway intermediates, validate products, and support structure-based interpretation of enzyme activity.

Transcriptomics & candidate mining

My approach often begins with expression data: transcriptome integration, coexpression analysis, and comparative patterns across cultivars or treatments to prioritize pathway candidates for functional study.

Plant defense metabolism

I study how plants deploy metabolic responses against pathogens, with emphasis on inducible defense metabolites, temporal responses after infection, and biosynthetic regulation under stress.
